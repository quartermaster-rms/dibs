# DIBS — service specification

**dibs** is QUARTERMASTER's equipment service: it manages a catalog of physical equipment, coordinates reservations against a shared calendar, energizes tools through hardware interlocks, enforces dual reserve/usage quotas, and carries user-filed issue reports that drive a public red/yellow/green health status. It reads identity and coarse group claims from Keycloak (README §2) and owns its fine-grained equipment permission model internally. Shared conventions — IDs, time, HTTP/error envelope, idempotency, health, audit, the interlock device protocol (§6), testing, deployment, UI — are defined in the [implementation guide](../IMPLEMENTATION-GUIDE.md) and only referenced here.

## Purpose & core invariants

Equipment is booked on an exclusive calendar for coordination, but the calendar never gates energizing a tool. Energizing (**Enable**) is decoupled from reservations: any principal at **user** tier or above for the item (or an `open_use` item) may Enable it whenever it is physically free. The overriding safety kernel (guide §6): **a running session is never ended automatically** — not at reservation end, not on quota exhaustion, not on a lost permission, not on a fatal fault, not during a service or IAM outage. The only mid-use stop is a deliberate manual force-close by an admin.

## Permissions

**Coarse (from the Keycloak token).** `sysadmin` — full access, overrides every internal tier. `admin-dibs` — the **admin tier**: the whole dibs administrative surface — configuring equipment, classes, quotas, nodes, and all runtime settings; the full issue lifecycle; removing another user's reservation; force-closing another user's session — plus final authority over superusers and the delegation model that governs them (below). `group-*` — department membership feeds the app-enforced **department gate**: an admin may require one or more groups to reach dibs or a given equipment class; `sysadmin` and `admin-dibs` bypass the gate. The admin tier is granted and revoked **only** in Keycloak (the external group), never from inside dibs.

**Fine — three tiers (owned by dibs, per equipment and per equipment class, evaluated per request, fail-closed; may be enforced with Cerbos).** All three are ordinary non-admin users: they differ only in what they may reserve/Enable and in the **admin-configured delegation abilities** their grant carries — every configuration, quota, issue-lifecycle, override, and session-closing authority still lives in the **admin** tier alone. A grant is scoped to a specific **item** or an entire **class**; a caller's effective tier on an item is the highest of its item grant and its class grant, defaulting to **none**:
- **none** — the default for every authorized user on every item and class. Read and report only: see all reachable equipment and its live status, view the reservation calendar and usage history, browse the People directory, and file or post updates to issue reports at any severity (but never close one). No reserve, no Enable.
- **user** — everything none can do, plus **reserve and Enable/operate** the scoped equipment, subject to the reserve and usage quotas.
- **superuser** — a **user** in every operational respect (identical reserve/Enable rights, same quotas) that additionally holds one or more **delegation abilities** an admin has enabled on its grant — *promote* (raise none→user), *grant superuser*, and/or *demote* — each exercised only within the grant's scope. It confers no configuration, session-closing, issue-lifecycle, override, or quota-exemption authority; those remain the admin tier's alone.

**Grant authority (configurable, defaults on Settings).** A superuser grant independently enables any of three delegation abilities — *promote* (none→user), *grant superuser*, *demote* — set by an admin and pre-filled from the app-wide defaults on the **Settings** page (conservative baseline: *promote* only). A superuser may use only the abilities its grant enables, always within scope, may **never confer an ability it does not itself hold** (no escalation), and may never affect an admin. Whether a *demote*-bearing superuser may demote a **peer** superuser in scope, or **itself**, is governed by admin-configurable settings (default off — only admins demote superusers). Admins hold every delegation ability unconditionally; the **admin** tier itself is assigned only in Keycloak, never inside dibs. `sysadmin` supersedes `admin-dibs` everywhere.

**Class expansion & open access.** dibs owns the item→class hierarchy, so an item check takes the higher of the item grant and its class grant; a class-level grant (or class-scoped superuser) therefore covers every item in the class, and access listings union item-level and class-expanded results. An **open-access** item (marked `open_use` at item *or* class level) confers effective **user** tier on any authenticated, department-gate-passing user with no explicit grant; quotas still apply.

## Data model (PostgreSQL; UUID PKs; `created_at`/`updated_at` throughout)

- **location** — `building`, `room`; unique together.
- **equipment_class** — `name` (unique), `description`, `department_groups[]` (class-level department gate), `open_use` (default false), `requires_enable` (default true) — the last two are class-wide settings the class's equipment combine with (below).
- **equipment** — `name`, `class_id`, `location_id`, `photo_path`, `requires_enable` (default true), `open_use` (default false), `qr_token` (deep-link slug). **Effective flags** combine item and class: an item is **open-access** if it *or* its class is marked `open_use`, and it is **enable-gated** only while both it *and* its class keep `requires_enable` (marking either as *no-enable* removes the Enable step entirely). A node links to exactly one item and may link only to enable-gated items (trigger-enforced); an item may carry any number of interlock nodes (0..N, no upper limit).
- **reservation** — `equipment_id`, `user_id` (subject), `created_by` (subject; always equals `user_id`), `starts_at`/`ends_at` (aligned to the configured reservation slot granularity — default 15 min, `ends_at>starts_at`, within advance limit), `status` (`booked`/`completed`/`cancelled`). A GiST exclusion constraint on the equipment × time range over non-cancelled rows enforces calendar exclusivity. Reservations are wholly independent of sessions — neither ever changes the other.
- **session** — `equipment_id`, `user_id`, `started_at`, `ended_at`, `end_cause` (`user`|`admin` — the only two ways a session ends). Partial unique index on `equipment_id WHERE ended_at IS NULL` guarantees at most one live session per item. The session table *is* the equipment usage history.
- **quota_policy** — `quota_type` (`reserve`|`usage`), `principal` (`user:<subject>`|`group:<id>`|`everyone`), `target_kind` (`item`|`class`), `target_id`, `window` (`day`|`week`|`month`), `limit_hours`, `hard_cap`, `active`.
- **issue_report** — `equipment_id`, `reporter_id`, `title`, `severity` (`warning`|`fatal` → yellow|red), `description`, `status` (`open`/`closed`), `closed_by`/`closed_at`. **issue_update** — the append-only comment/history trail: `issue_id`, `author_id`, `body`, `created_at`; a report's *last update* is the latest of these, and any authenticated user may add one. **issue_photo** — optional attachment (`issue_id`, `update_id?`, `path`).
- **interlock_node** — `equipment_id` (an equipment may have any number of nodes, 0..N; not unique), `key_hash` (+ `prev_key_hash`/expiry for rotation), `fail_state`, `poll_interval_s`, `heartbeat_interval_s`, `enabled` (admin kill), `last_heartbeat_at`, `last_firmware`, `offline`.
- **principal** — directory identity cached at login (`subject` PK, `display_name`, `email`, coarse `groups[]`, `is_admin`, `last_seen_at`); backs the People directory and grant rosters — dibs never queries the external directory. **role_grant** — one fine tier grant: `subject`, `scope_kind` (`item`|`class`), `scope_id`, `tier` (`user`|`superuser`), `granted_by`, and — for superuser grants — the delegation flags `can_promote`/`can_grant_superuser`/`can_demote` (defaulted from settings); unique on `(subject, scope_kind, scope_id)`, and its absence *is* the none tier. **setting** (`key`/`value`) — the Settings page is backed by these plus `quota_policy`; plus idempotency records per the guide. **audit** — an append-only record of **every action** (enable/disable, reserve/cancel, promote/demote/grant, issue file/update/close, equipment/class/location/quota/node/setting CRUD, force-close, and any other state change): `ts`, `actor`, `action`, `object_type`, `object_id`, `before`, `after`, `request_id`; never updated or deleted; surfaced to admins on a dedicated Audit page. The per-equipment **usage history** (session start/stop events) is the user-visible subset.

## Key behaviors

**Reservations (good-faith, fully detached from Enable).** The calendar coordinates intent only; dibs never checks whether a reservation is used and never Enables, Disables, or frees anything on a reservation's behalf. Reservations align to the configured **reservation slot granularity** (default 15 min), minimum one slot, no further ahead than the configured **max reservation days in advance**. A reservation is **cancelable — and freely movable/resizable — strictly before its `starts_at`**; once `starts_at` passes it is immutable and is swept to `completed` at `ends_at`. There are **no** penalties for last-minute bookings or no-shows and no grace or no-show reconciliation of any kind. A modify is an atomic cancel+rebook quota-checked as the replacement. dibs never auto-frees a slot. No principal — admins included — may create or modify a reservation on another user's behalf (**no impersonation**); a reservation's `user_id` is always its creator. **Removing (cancelling) another user's reservation is an admin-only moderation action.**

**Enable / sessions.** Enable applies only to **enable-gated** equipment (a *no-enable* item has no Enable path and no session at all). Preconditions, first failure returns its code: caller active (else 403); effective **user** tier or above on the item or its class — which every user has on an **open-access** item (else 403); item physically free — the same holder re-Enabling gets the running session idempotently, another holder gets 409 `equipment_in_use` (never force-ended); no open fatal issue — red blocks all users but admits **admins** for diagnosis (else 422 `fatal_fault_open`); usage-quota remaining > 0 (else 422 `quota_exceeded`). Success creates the session and drives all of the item's interlock nodes to desired-state true; it never touches any reservation. A node's `enabled=false` **admin kill** blocks new Enables on that node's equipment but never ends a live session and never de-energizes one already running. **Disable** ends the session only via the holder ending their own (`user`) or an **admin** force-closing another's (`admin`); nothing else ever ends it.

**Dual quotas (admin-set on the Settings page).** Two independent limits, each enforced strictly at the point of action and never mid-session. **Reserve** quota is checked when booking/modifying: a user's consumption is simply the **summed duration of all their non-cancelled reservations** in the window (`booked` or `completed`, the full booked interval) — cancelled reservations never count and no-shows are never reconciled. **Usage** quota is checked at Enable only, against summed prior-session time; a running session may exceed the cap and keeps running. Precedence per type: user beats group, item beats class, ties take the lowest limit; every `hard_cap` policy also applies. Admins are always exempt; users and superusers are quota'd identically.

**Issue reports & derived status.** Any authenticated user **files** an issue (title, severity, description) and any authenticated user may **post updates** to it — the trail records each update's author and timestamp — but **only an admin may close** one. Reports and their full history (open and closed) are readable by every authenticated user, no grant needed. Equipment status color is computed, never stored: **red** if any open fatal, else **yellow** if any open warning, else **green**; returned with per-severity open counts on every equipment response. Red is the single removal-from-service mechanism — planned maintenance is just a fatal issue conventionally titled "Maintenance," filed like any other; closing the last open issue of a color restores it. Filing or updating an issue never touches a running session; stopping a machine mid-use is exclusively a manual admin force-close. Equipment stays bookable on red.

## HTTP API (under `/api/`; user plane, additive extensions allowed)

- `GET /me`, `GET /me/quota?equipment_id=` (per-window remaining, both types), `GET /me/reservations`.
- `GET /locations`, `GET /classes`, `GET /equipment?q&class_id&location_id&authorized&enabled_by_me` — the main list: `q` matches equipment name, class name, and location; `authorized` limits to items the caller may Enable, `enabled_by_me` to items the caller currently holds. Every row carries the equipment name, computed status + per-severity open counts, the **caller's effective tier**, the current session holder (if any), and the next upcoming reservation. `GET /equipment/{id}` adds location, class, and the full issue and reservation data; `GET /equipment/{id}/history` is the usage history (past sessions: holder, start, end); `GET /equipment/by-qr/{qr_token}`. All public read.
- `GET /equipment/{id}/reservations?from&to`, `POST /equipment/{id}/reservations` (**user** tier; always for the caller; `Idempotency-Key`), `PATCH /reservations/{id}`, `DELETE /reservations/{id}` (cancel — permitted strictly before `starts_at`; an **admin** may cancel another user's).
- `POST /equipment/{id}/enable` → session (enable-gated items only), `POST /equipment/{id}/disable` (own session; an **admin** force-closes another's).
- `POST /equipment/{id}/issues` (file — any user: title, severity, description), `POST /issues/{id}/updates` (add an update — any user), `POST /issues/{id}/photos` (multipart), `GET /equipment/{id}/issues?include_closed&severity&q`, `GET /issues?status&severity&equipment_id&location_id`, `GET /issues/{id}` (full description + update trail) — all public read; `POST /issues/{id}/close` (**admin** only — else 403).
- **Tiers** (fine grants): `GET /equipment/{id}/grants` and `GET /classes/{id}/grants` list the user/superuser holders of a scope (searchable; admins hold no per-scope grant and aren't listed). `PUT /grants` (`{subject, scope_kind ∈ item|class, scope_id, tier ∈ none|user|superuser}` plus the superuser delegation flags) applies the transition against the caller's **configured delegation abilities**: the caller must hold *promote* to raise none→user, *grant superuser* to set superuser, or *demote* to lower a tier — and may never confer an ability it lacks or affect an admin (else 403 `grant_forbidden`). Admins may do all of it; the admin tier is never set here.
- `GET /people` — the **People directory** (any authenticated user): every dibs-known user (anyone who has signed in to dibs or holds a fine grant) with their SSO name and email and their dibs standing (admin, or their notable equipment/class tiers).
- **Settings / admin** (`admin-dibs`/`sysadmin`): the Settings surface — superuser delegation-ability defaults, usage & reserve **quota-policy** CRUD, **max reservation days in advance**, open-access/no-enable defaults, department-gate config, reservation slot granularity, superuser peer/self-demote permissions — plus entity management: CRUD locations/classes/equipment (with each item's and class's `open_use` and `requires_enable`/no-enable flags) and node provisioning (`POST /nodes` returns the one-time key, `POST /nodes/{id}/rotate-key`, `PATCH /nodes/{id}`), `GET /analytics/utilization?class_id&from&to`, and `GET /audit?actor&action&object_type&object_id&from&to` — the full append-only action log, paginated.
- **Device plane** — the shared interlock protocol (guide §6) on a separate TLS port; nodes poll desired-state and heartbeat.

## UI scope

React SPA served at `/` (guide §9), deep-link fallback to `index.html`, unknown `/api/*` stay JSON 404s. Everything centers on equipment; every screen is readable at any permission level, and admin-only controls appear inline where relevant.

**Equipment list (home).** One giant scrollable list of every reachable piece of equipment, shown to every user regardless of tier. A search/filter bar matches equipment name, class name, and location, plus two toggles — *equipment I'm authorized to* and *equipment I currently have enabled*. Each equipment is a row that prominently shows: the name; a colored status indicator (green = good, yellow = nonfatal issue, red = fatal issue); the caller's own access level to that item; whether anyone is enabled and, if so, who; an Enable/Disable button (absent on *no-enable* items, and blocked with an explanation when another user holds it); and the next reservation.

**Equipment detail.** Everything from the row, plus the equipment's location and its class; a searchable, filterable list of current and past issues; a Google-Calendar-style reservation calendar with interactive booking (drag on desktop, tap on mobile) in which a user manages their own reservations and **only an admin can remove another's**; a "file a new issue" button; the equipment usage history (past sessions); and a **searchable roster of everyone at user or superuser tier on this equipment or its class** (admins have implicit access and aren't listed). A caller whose grant enables *promote* sees a promote panel with a username selector; if it also enables *grant superuser*, a promote-to-superuser option appears; if it enables *demote*, a demote control appears on listed people — each shown only to those configured for it. An **admin-only Interlocks section** on this page lets admins add, configure, rotate-key, kill, and remove this equipment's interlock nodes (0..N); it is hidden from anyone without node-management permission.

**Issues.** Each row in the issue list shows the title, filed date/time, last-updated date/time, severity (yellow or red), and the user who filed it. Opening an issue adds its full description and the complete update history — every update with its date, time, and author. Any user may file an issue or post an update; **closing an issue is admin-only** and surfaced only to admins.

**People.** A top-level People page lists every dibs-known user and admin (those who have signed in or hold a grant) with their dibs permission standing and their email (name and email are guaranteed by the SSO provider), readable by any user. It is a directory only; all admin configuration lives on the Settings page.

**Settings (admin only).** A dedicated Settings page is the single home for every runtime policy: the superuser delegation-ability defaults; usage and reserve quotas; the max reservation days in advance; the open-access and no-enable defaults; the reservation slot granularity; the superuser peer/self-demote permissions; the department gate; and entity management — rooms, equipment classes, and individual equipment (with their open-access / no-enable flags). Interlock nodes are provisioned per-equipment in an admin-only section on the equipment page, not here. A dedicated **Audit** page (admin only) lists every logged action with filters (actor, action, object, time). Per-equipment tier grants are made on the equipment page; the reciprocal admin overrides (force-closing any user's session, closing issues, removing others' reservations) live inline on the equipment and issue screens.

**Capability-aware controls (app-wide).** If you are not qualified for an action, its control is hidden or at least greyed out — never a live button that only fails on click. This holds everywhere: Enable/Disable, reserve, promote/grant-superuser/demote, close-issue, and every admin control.

In-app help on every friction point (guide §9).

## Deployment

A self-contained Docker Compose stack (guide §8) built from this repo, run non-root: **api**, **worker** (node-offline detection — never any session re-evaluation), **scheduler** (reservation-completion sweep — no no-show or grace sweeps), and the **device** container terminating its own TLS, plus this service's own **postgres** and **redis** on an internal network. Only the api (loopback) and device port are published; volumes hold Postgres and the uploads directory. All deployment values come from `deploy/host.env` (real Keycloak/TLS in production, the hermetic stub-login and placeholders in dev/test — the only path difference). Failed-deploy recovery per the guide.
